Justice is Outdated

Article 003, 2023/1/15


The Social Idea of Justice

Justice is society's way of making somebody pay for their crimes. Justice means retribution. It's the idea that someone deserves what they give to others. But what does that do for anyone? If a serial murderer is caught, should they be tortured as a way for them to pay for their crimes? What good does that do anyone? Should they be put to death?

The Political Idea of Justice

The Problem of a Biased Eye-for-an-eye View

The central problem with justice is that it is entirely subjective. Revenge and an-eye-for-an-eye are even more problematic, since they allow everyone to be a vigilante, punishing "crimes" however they see fit. Take, as an example, the view of a [specific type of] school shooter. Let's say they are heavily bullied and outcast from the cliches at school. It is likely that they will imagine ways of getting revenge on their bullies. In the most benign circumstance, it might just be telling teachers about it to get them to stop it. But in most cases the student has already done that and it hasn't worked, or maybe it has even worsened it. So they imagine ways that they themselves can stop it. That is how a school shooter happens. By allowing the outdated eye-for-an-eye view to pervade our courts and legal systems, it also invades all of our minds and allows us to think that revenge is fair and natural.

The school shooter example is why revenge is not a good concept. When faced with continual abuse, people will think of wild, completely immoral actions in order to stop it. Revenge allows the victim to act out their anger on their abusers, often in ways that when viewed from an outside perspective seem extreme.

In this view of an eye-for-an-eye, self-defense can be seen as a form of justice, since it is a form of vigilantism, which itself is a form of revenge. If someone breaks into your home, your defense of yourself and your home is a direct response to their break-inâ€”it is an act of revenge for their intrusion into your home, as well as an act of self-preservation to ensure the safety of your own life.

This is where justice is acceptable. Justice is acceptable if it prevents a direct and immediate danger to your life, as is the case in the home intrusion example. On the other hand, shooting up a school because you have been bullied in the past is obviously not an acceptable form of justice, because the bullying was in the past and there is no immediate danger to your life. The bullying is an explanation, but it is no excuse. However, many schools don't view self-defense as a valid application of justice. Many schools have a policy called the no-tolerance policy, which says that a victim is not allowed to fight back and should seek an authority to enact punishment on the bully. This is a failure by the schools.

Justice is Outdated

We have now established that Justice is acceptable if it prevents a direct and immediate danger to one's life. I believe that this is this the one and only case where justice is acceptable. In all other cases, justice is an outdated notion and rehabilitation should be tried instead. The reason that I think this is because justice doesn't help anything, especially the perpetrator. It locks them away; out of sight, out of mind.
