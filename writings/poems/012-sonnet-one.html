Sonnet I

Poem 012, 2023/3/7


My heart is heavy,
‘cause still I know you’re just the same—
“Same as it ever was, same as it ever will be”,
yet I’m the one that’s changed.

I know it’s not your fault—
no, the fault lies all with me.
Still for you, my heart beats, and you my only thought:
lie echoes of a love set free.

And in that void I call my soul,
there is nothing but a thread of my sorrows
which I wrap in memories of all the things to me you told;
nothing left now, but “tomorrows”.

So spare me of all that heartache, God, and the despair too—
Give me a life worth living, so I can see it through.
