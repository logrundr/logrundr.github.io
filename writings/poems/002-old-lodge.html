A Stay in the Old Lodge

Poem 002, 2022/5/22–24, updated 2022/10/11


Chapter 1

The old lodge has:
long wallpapered corridors;
taps on the windows as
thunderous rain downpours;

halls lined with lanterns,
lights of crystal spheres;
lobby fireplace burns,
light cast by chandeliers;

dorms of small beds,
with clothes-stuffed wardrobes;
lying sleepy-heads,
in their night robes.

My chamber furnished:
walls of floral design;
windows with curtains faded,
outside creep vines;

pictures hanging down,
of people long gone;
Persian rugs strewn around,
beautiful patterns drawn;


Chapter 2

I lie awake in bed when,
in glee, my love calls me;
now I’m leaving my den,
not forgetting my key.
