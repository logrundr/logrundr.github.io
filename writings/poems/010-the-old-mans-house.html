The Old Man's House

Poem 010, 2023/2/28


I moved into the house of a recently dead old man.
I sometimes see his face, in a plant,
or in my hand,
or on my TV stand—

And then I wonder if he’s there, watching over me.
Stepping into a cold spot: “Go away!” I plea,
“You’re not here!
“You’re outside, buried last year…!”
