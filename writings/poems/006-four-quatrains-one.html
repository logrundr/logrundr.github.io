Four Quatrains I

Poem 006, 2023/1/28–2/15


I (2023/01/28)
If thee aren’t the fairest of all on Earth,
then I shall ask if God walks among men;
For surely men that stand with the most in worth,
deserve instead of fortune, wealth in zen, then.


II (2023/01/30)
The tranquil of minds which are shroud in love
are always better measured against hate;
The former moves the heavenly stars above,
the latter nothing, ‘cept to divide and tempt fate.


III (2023/02/07)
All things have their end and are born again,
so wishing you were young is an undoing.
To waste all your life is the sin of man,
so love your Self in happiness pursuing.


IV (2023/02/15)
If peace is what you’re after,
then give no mind to things without control;
Instead they’re met with laughter,
the only way to meet your nature’s goal.
