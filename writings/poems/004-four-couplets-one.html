Four Couplets I

Poem 004, 2023/1/18–22


I (2023/01/18)
A bard plucks the lute, and birds chirp the sad melody.
As he recites his poem: the birds all go mute, and listen to his elegy.


III (2023/01/20)
Every person is a friend if you’re kind enough;
but take care to never offend in blind rebuff.


IV (2023/01/22)
A driven car obeys the driver’s command;
but a horse being ridden will do anything to be unmanned.


V (2023/01/22)
The train cuts through the forest, whistle blowing,
while the animals stare bewildered from their home in Wyoming
