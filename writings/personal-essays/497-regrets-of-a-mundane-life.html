Regrets of a Mundane Life

Entry 497, 2021/5/14


I know it's been a while since I've last written. I just spent 8 hours today reading a book from start to finish, cover to cover, and am in a sort of mood now that can only happen after you're done reading a good book. It's a sort of wistfulness and regret that I feel because I don't live in their world, and I never will. It makes me feel sad that my world is so plain and boring, and theirs is so exciting and mysterious.

My life will never be as thrilling as the characters within a book. I think that someday I would like to write my own novels.

I'm just now coming out of the Dark Ages of my life, which I would say comprised late-7th grade up to a bit beyond the end of 12th grade—essentially, starting at my breakup with my first girlfriend and ending at my breakup with my second.

She was there for me during my Dark Ages, and for that I am grateful. She helped me through it somehow, even though I know that she never meant to.

My Golden Ages were from 5th grade up until 7th grade. Those were the best years of my life in many ways, and I will never be able to get it back—not anything. There are certain feelings and emotions that surround those years that I suspect I will never feel again. It's simply a period of my life that has now passed, but I can still vaguely relive it through my own memories, however accurate they may be.

It's the same way for my Dark Ages. I occasionally dipped very low... to the point of wanting to end my life at several moments. I think that time has gone now, too. I would certainly hope that it has, but it has made up a considerable portion of my short life so far, and because of that, it has affected me in ways that I am still living today, if only because I only came out of it a year ago.

It is a coincidence that my best years were followed by my worst? Is it going to be a recurring pattern throughout my life that my highest highs are followed by my lowest lows? That a great period must be followed by a terrible one, as if by some kind of balance? Will I ever have years that I cannot classify as great or terrible, but simply as "mundane"—or is mundanity always assumed to be terrible?
