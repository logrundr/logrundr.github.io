Any Meaning, and if one needs to exist

Entry 463, 2021/2/10


I like my journal because it gives me a medium to express my thoughts. I don't like having my thoughts just confined within my head, because it makes me feel like I'm alone, or trapped within myself. Seeing my ideas written out here feels good, and it's interesting to look back on earlier entries to see what I was thinking back then.

I've had some difficulty finding a meaning in life. Truly (technically), there is no meaning to life—but if I was talking purely emotionally, then I would say that a meaning to life exists. This is probably what most people do when they try to find a meaning. I try to look at an overall universal purpose, but most people just look to themselves (which is probably the smarter move, since you are really all that you have).

I guess the meaning to life is just to do what fulfills you. What counts as fulfilling will be different from person to person. What fulfills me specifically is music. I love music. It is my great emotional outlet. I'm interested in a lot of other things too. I like thinking about how a society functions: that's why I like creating languages, thinking about time systems, calendar systems, alphabets, grammar structures, social hierarchies, governments, etc. It's fun to think about societies and how they would work. Bringing that idea to a broader scale makes me think about how the universe works.

Everybody has something that they like to do. It might be something that damages others, or hurts themself. Those types of people wouldn't be considered good people, but it could be said that they are fulfilling themselves and therefore fulfilling their purpose.

Some people say that everything happens for a reason—and it's true... things do happen for a reason. They happen as consequences from other things... that is the reason for them happening. Whether the ultimate outcome is good or not entirely depends on what comes before it. Things don't come out of nowhere—there is always a cause.

How does that logic apply to the universe? Is there a cause for the existence of the universe? What actions caused the universe to be created? Like I've stated in the past, the universe defies a lot of conventional logic. The universe is infinite, like I've also said. Infinity is a hard thing to comprehend. In fact, it is impossible except on a conceptual level.

I would say that the universe has no meaning, technically. Life is here just because it can be. There is no other reason. As intelligent life, we get to make our own meanings and live them out. They don't have any ultimate significance, though—maybe that's a good thing. Maybe it's good that we don't matter because then we can do whatever we want without worrying what we will affect.
