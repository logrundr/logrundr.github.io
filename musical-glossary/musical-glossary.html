Musical Glossary


Double Canon

two canons played at the same time that work with each other. A double two-part canon, for example, is two two-part canons, each with its own melodic line, played ontop of each other, making four-part counterpoint. See Igor Stravinsky's double two-part canon.

Intriguer

a piece with a certain atmosphere (usually of playful mystery) and uniqueness, such that it intrigues the listener to find other works by that composer. Usually intriguers tend to be the more famous of a composer's works; i.e. Franz Liszt's Hungarian Rhapsody N. 2 or La Campanella.

Logrundr

a log being a canon, and grund being the Old English word for ground; a logrundr is a log (a canon) played over a ground, a repeating bassline. Coined by Moondog, who composed several logrundrs.

Philosophy Song

a song with lyrics of profound meaning, typically relating to philosophical or spiritual ideas about the Universe, and ideas about the nature and human experience of life.

Utterance

a certain string of mantras which is repeated as a pattern within the music. The mantras can be seen as words or short phrases which make up the larger sentence or paragraph of the utterance, which is repeated and can have variations in the repeats as well as improvisatory interludes.
