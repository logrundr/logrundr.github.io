Consistent Calendar (B01):
Also categorised as Aodhan Calendar, and its Conversion Calendars

Length: 365 days

Leap years: every 4 years, except if the year is a multiple of 100 that is also not a multiple of 400

73 weeks of 5 days each: Monday, Tuesday, Wendsday, Thursday, Friday
Note: Wednesday is respelled as Wendsday

The work week is of 3 days working--on Monday, Tuesday, and Wendsday--and 2 days resting-- Thursday and Friday. A ratio of 3:2 compared to the standard 5:2.

5 months of 73 days each: Novensis, Primensis, Dumensis, Trimensis, Tandemensis

The month names are a combination of the Latin words for Month and for New, First, Second, Third, and Last.

On leap years, the leap day is placed at the beginning of the year, and is called New Day. It is not part of any month or day of the week. The day after New Day is Monday, Novensis 1, as it is in every year.

The day of the week for any given date is always the same for any year, unlike our system where the days of the week shift over every year.

The 20th of every month is Game Day, which is a holiday for playing board games and other games with family and friends.

Month Respelling (B01A):
Proposed 2022, Sept. 2

Proposed respelling of the 5 months to:
Noven, Primen, Dumen, Tremen, Tandemen

Reasoning:

  1. Removes unnecessary “sis” at the end of each month name
  2. Respelled “Trimen” to “Tremen” to distinguish it more clearly from “Primen”
